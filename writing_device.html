2019 Health and Regenerative Medicine Hackathon

Project Background

As part of the Victoria 2019 Health Hackathon, we were tasked with designing a terminal devices to assist in everyday tasks. We designed a device to hold a pencil to make writing easier for children at school by addressing grip issues with holding a pencil with the prosthetic hand. Our deviceintegrates into the hand and fits into the 3D printing infrastructure that is deployed in clinics in 8 countries around the world.

The Device

The writing aid consists of 2 3D-printed components. As part of the first component, there is a rectangular "ring" that was designed to fit around the VHP children's prosthetic hand. For adjustment and sliding on/removing the device, there is a tightening screww at the bottom.

Secondly, as part of the finger ring, there is a gear with "teeth" at 60 deg increments that are used for rotating the penciil angle. Moreover, with a hole on the top of the gear, once the ideal position is found, a pin can be used to locl the position.

The second component interfaces with the pencil or pen. This component slides onto the finger ring using the gear-shaped attachment interface. Finally, there is a hole for inserting the pencil into and locking it in with a screw that drives normal to the pencil.
